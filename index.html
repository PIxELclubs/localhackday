About

What is Local Hack Day Santa Ana?

In partnership with GitHub and Major League Hacking, PIxEL (a student-run technology education nonprofit in Orange County) is hosting a 12-hour coding event for all students interested in learning programming or software engineering.

At Local Hack Day Santa Ana, we will be holding workshops and activities, providing lunch and dinner, and giving students access to mentorship as they learn or work on projects throughout the course of the event. Whether you are a complete beginner or have been coding for five years, there is a place for you at Local Hack Day.

We will also be providing a limited number of computers for use during the event for students that do not have a computer they are able to bring.

Frequently Asked Questions

What do I bring to Local Hack Day Santa Ana?

A computer and its charger are the only essentials: you are also welcome to bring your own food if you have dietary restrictions. We will be serving lunch and dinner and providing snacks and water throughout the event.

What if I don't have a computer?

We will have a limited number of computers that you are able to use if you do not have access to a home or school computer to bring. Just let us know that you need a computer in the Special Notes section of registration and we will set you up on the day of!

Is there a code of conduct?

We want to make sure that Local Hack Day events create a safe and inspirational environment for attendees and organizers. For this reason the MLH Code of Conduct should be followed throughout the duration of each event.
